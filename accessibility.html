Overview

It seems great when we experience the web content on multiple portals or access products that help resolve our day to day problems. However, the scenario has changed and now the scope is not limited to the sighted users. Developers have to consider people with special abilities. All the applications should be accessible to non-sighted users, people with motor abilities, people having hearing or cognitive impairments.

As technology makes inroads into newer domains and applications, making products accessible for one and all has also become the need of the day. For instance, USA has defined standards like Section 508, Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) to handle the audience with special abilities.

Section 508 testing, more specifically WCAG2.1 compliance testing/development is important to standardize accessibility engineering in product development. WCAG Compliance is one of our core focus areas in the accessibility development services that we provide.

Front End Accessible Development

Web accessibility is gaining momentum. However, significant portions of the population are unable to access the Internet by traditional means because of the visual, hearing, motor or cognitive disabilities.

With an experience of more than a decade in accessibility development, we can do Front end development/ redesign existing application our development team will implement an accessible solution with minimal efforts.

Why Accessibility?

Our E2E development services assure to include all necessary alt attributes on images, and spare customer listening to a screen reader speaking gibberish for the missing (or inaccurate) alt text.

Our technical expertise provides clean and accurate development tricks that reliably help visually impaired a lot in receiving accurate information.

Tools We Use

JAWS is a computer screen reader program for that allows visually impaired users to read the screen either with a text-to-speech output or by a refreshable Braille display.

NonVisual Desktop Access is a free, open-source, portable screen reader for Microsoft Windows.

VoiceOver is a screen reader built into Apple Inc.'s macOS, iOS, tvOS, watchOS, and iPod operating systems.

Accessibility Testing

An Independent testing team is involved in scanning the applications to find out accessibility issues complaint to ‘A’, ‘AA’ or ‘AAA’ standards. They usually follow a paired approach(i.e one sighted & one non-sighted tester) to report accessibility bugs.

Accessibility testing procedure is a consolidated manual and automation process. While there are accessibility testing tools that can enable you to decide whether a site finishes the automated WCAG tests, regardless it requires manual connection to collaborate WCAG 2.0 rules.
